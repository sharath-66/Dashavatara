Rama Avatar

The Story of RAMA Avatar


In Rama Avatar, Lord Vishnu incarnates himself as Rama, the central character in the epic Ramayan.
In the epic, the character Rama is expected to show the world the characteristics of an ideal person, including ideal son, ideal husband, ideal king and an ideal person.
RAMA was incarnated upon this planet to get rid of the asura with ten heads, Ravana, who had been granted a boon by Brahma of immunity from gods, and other celestial beings.
Ravana was too vainglorious to be thinking of being vanquished by a man.
Hence RAMA was born and Lakshmi, wife of Lord Vishnu was born as Sita, his wife to be in this life.
The story of Ramayan, is an exciting nailbiting story of the war raged by Rama against various evil elements of the world and in the end against Ravana.

Ramayan epitomises the ideal behaviour of man, with special focus on man-wife relationship, son-father relationship and the rules for ideal governance by a king.

Analysis:
Here Ramayan, an epic signifies us that however God worshipping you are you'll be destroyed if you aren't a follower of Dharma
In this epic, Ramayan, Ravana was a great worshipper of Lord Shiva,
he had most respected "MAHA PATHIVRATA" Mandodhari as his wife,
he had many boons from many lords including from Shiva, Brahma to say a few,
He had a huge army
he was the king who had won all the TRILOKA's,
he knew the usage of almost all the weapons that existed at that time
and most importantly he was a MAHA BRAHMANA who knew about past, present and also future(i.e., He was a TRIKALA Gnani).
But dispite all of such epitomy he died by the hands of Lord Rama because he had abducted Rama's wife Sita and this ultimately led to his destruction.
Epic Ramayan and Rama Avatar signifies us that --------> we should be very much satisfied by whatever is given to us by God, because he gives it for a reason, and we must not feel wanting too much.
Here Ravana couldn't have died if he hadn't had abducted Sita and kept her captivated.
Ravan was very jealous of Sita's beauty, eventhough he had a very beautiful wife who was also very obedient to him.

Moral Analysis:
Sita and Hanuman are metaphors for the strength we possess,
Ravan on the other hand is the metaphor for the weakness we possess,
